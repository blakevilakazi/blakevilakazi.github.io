What Are Cookies?

Cookies are small text files placed on your device when you visit a website. They allow websites to recognise your device, remember your preferences, maintain your login session, and improve your experience.

Cookies do not normally access other files on your device but may collect identifiers such as your IP address, browser type, or device type.

Legal Framework

This Cookie Policy is designed to comply with applicable South African legislation, including:

  • Protection of Personal Information Act 4 of 2013 (POPIA)
  • Electronic Communications and Transactions Act 25 of 2002 (ECTA)
  • Consumer Protection Act 68 of 2008 (where applicable)

Under POPIA, certain cookies may constitute processing of personal information where they identify or can reasonably identify a user.

4

Third-Party Cookies

Some features on SQUIDS may use third-party services that set their own cookies. These include:

  • Authentication providers — if you use social login options.
  • Analytics providers — for anonymous usage reporting.
  • AI service providers — when using AI-powered features.

We require all third-party providers to comply with applicable privacy laws. However, their cookie usage is governed by their own privacy policies.

5

Managing Your Cookie Preferences

You can control and manage cookies through your browser settings. Most browsers allow you to:

  • View all cookies currently set.
  • Delete all or specific cookies.
  • Block third-party cookies.
  • Set preferences for specific websites.

Please note that disabling essential cookies will prevent you from logging in and using key features of SQUIDS.

For browser-specific instructions, visit your browser's help centre. Disabling cookies on your device will apply across all websites, not just SQUIDS.
