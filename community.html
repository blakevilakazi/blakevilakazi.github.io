Community Standards

Community Guidelines

Last updated: 27 February 2026
For all SQUIDS users

"SQUIDS is built on the belief that Africa deserves a social platform where ideas surface freely — but freedom comes with responsibility. These guidelines define what that means in practice."

— The SQUIDS Team

Why These Guidelines Exist

SQUIDS is a place for genuine conversation, debate, questions, and ideas. These guidelines exist to ensure that everyone — regardless of background, opinion, or identity — can participate without fear of harassment, abuse, or harm.

These guidelines apply to all content on SQUIDS, including posts, comments, messages, AI interactions, usernames, and profile information. Violations may result in content removal, account suspension, or permanent bans.

See also: Terms §9: Community Rules Terms §10: Moderation

Content Standards

Encouraged
  • Ask genuine questions
  • Share thoughtful opinions
  • Debate ideas respectfully
  • Credit original creators
  • Post original content
  • Support fellow users
Not Allowed
  • Illegal content of any kind
  • Hate speech or discrimination
  • Violence or threats
  • Spam or repetitive posts
  • Misleading misinformation
  • Sexual content involving minors

Harassment & Abuse — Zero Tolerance

SQUIDS has a zero-tolerance policy for harassment and abuse. The following are strictly prohibited:

  • Targeted harassment or bullying of any user.
  • Threats of physical violence or harm.
  • Doxxing — sharing someone's private personal information without consent.
  • Stalking or persistently contacting someone who has blocked you.
  • Coordinated attacks on a specific person or group.

Reports of harassment are investigated immediately. Accounts found in violation will be suspended pending review and may be permanently banned.

Report: support@squids.co.za How to Report

Hate Speech & Discrimination

Content that promotes hatred, discrimination, or dehumanisation based on any of the following is prohibited:

  • Race, ethnicity, or national origin
  • Religion or religious beliefs
  • Gender, gender identity, or sexual orientation
  • Disability or health status
  • Age or immigration status

This applies to both explicit hate speech and coded language designed to dehumanise groups. Violations are subject to immediate content removal and account action under the Promotion of Equality and Prevention of Unfair Discrimination Act (PEPUDA).

Misinformation & Fake Content

SQUIDS takes the spread of false information seriously. Do not post:

  • Deliberately false health or safety information.
  • Fabricated quotes attributed to real people.
  • Manipulated media designed to deceive.
  • False claims about elections, voting, or government processes.

Satirical content is permitted but must be clearly labelled as satire.

AI-Generated Content

You are responsible for any AI-generated content you post on SQUIDS. You may not use AI to:

  • Generate content that violates any other section of these guidelines.
  • Impersonate a real person or organisation.
  • Mass-produce spam or low-quality posts.
  • Create synthetic media designed to deceive.

AI-generated content should be clearly disclosed where it could mislead other users about its origins.

See: Terms §7: AI Services

Protection of Minors

The protection of children is our highest priority. The following is absolutely prohibited and will be reported to law enforcement:

  • Child sexual abuse material (CSAM) of any kind.
  • Grooming behaviour targeting minors.
  • Content that sexualises minors in any way.
  • Content encouraging minors to engage in dangerous behaviour.

All reports of child exploitation will be escalated to the South African Police Service and the Film and Publication Board (FPB) without delay.

How to Report a Violation

If you see content that violates these guidelines, please report it:

  • In-app: Use the report button on any post, comment, or profile.
  • Email: support@squids.co.za with details and screenshots.
  • Urgent safety issues: Mark your email subject line "URGENT — Safety Report".

All reports are reviewed by our moderation team. We take every report seriously and will update you on the outcome where possible.
